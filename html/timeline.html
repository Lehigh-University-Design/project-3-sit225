Ancient Lore

Eons ago an ancient civilization, named The Ancients, existed in a similar manner to modern humanity. They lived in a Buddhist-influenced dogmatic society heavily influenced by The Great Cycle. This cycle was viewed as a prison as it was an everlasting pattern of death and rebirth, never allowing a soul to truly leave the world. The Ancients embarked on a quest for transcendence, aiming to break free of this cycle. They identified five fundamental 'natural urges' as obstacles to achieving this higher state of being. These natural urges were violence, lust, material possession/attachment, gluttony, and self-preservation. To overcome these obstacles, the Ancients turned to archaic methods, seeking to rid themselves of attachments and desires.

However, these early attempts proved imperfect, prompting the civilization to explore alternative paths. Deep below the surface in the Depths, the Ancients discovered the Void Sea. The Void Sea was made up of Void Fluid and offered the potential to transcend without relying on traditional methods. However, there was a risk associated with taking the plunge; the possibility of evolving into an Echo. Echoes are eldritch entities who have partially transcended, but are bound due to some strong attachment to the world. These uncertainties lead to the development of Iterators.

Iterators, colossal artificial superintelligences, were tasked with solving the "Great Problem": finding a method of breaking free from the Great Cycle without the use of Void Fluid. These machines were constructed using a combination of organic and synthetic components and were built to stand above the clouds. However, in order for Iterators to keep their components cool and organic components clean, they required an immense amount of water. The Iterators stripped their nearby ecosystems of water and pumped water vapor back into the atmosphere, where it would condense into rain and be used again. Due to the torrential and deadly downpours caused by Iterators, the Ancients built their cities on top of the surface of the Iterators' exterior.

The civilization of the Ancients vanished and crumbled before the Iterators could unveil the solution to the Great Problem. A monumental movement towards transcendence swept through the population, leading to the abandonment of surface cities, which now stand as silent monuments to a bygone era. Despite the collapse of their creators, the Iterators remained operational, tasked with an enduring command: to persist in their pursuit of transcendence without reliance on Void Fluid. They were entrusted with the solemn duty of guiding lesser beings along the path to the ethereal realm, should the solution ever be discovered.

Yet, there was one important rule that an Iterator could not intentionally disobey; they cannot destroy themselves before finding a solution.

The Great Problem

Iterators continue their attempts to solve the Great Problem without any major breakthroughs.

Sliver of Straw

An Iterator named Sliver of Straw claims to have found a solution, but dies before confirming it, leading to her methods becoming lost.

Erratic Pulse

Five Pebbles becomes convinced there is no solution and contemplates destroying himself, he expresses these thoughts in Iterator chat logs under the pseudonym EP, or Erratic Pulse.

Taboo

Five Pebbles attempts to shuffle his genetics so much so that it would result in the self-destruction taboo to be erased. However, in order to complete, this task required several times the usual amount of water.

Looks to the Moon

Five Pebbles begins drawing excessive amounts of water, endangering his sister Iterator, Looks to the Moon by draining her of their shared water source.

Blocked

Moon attempts to persuade Pebbles to stop, but Pebbles has blocked all incoming communications in order to focus on his task.

Seven Red Suns

During this silent period from Five Pebbles, another Iterator named Seven Red Suns, sends a messenger to deliver a physical message to Five Pebbles in an attempt to stop him from harming Looks to the Moon any more than he already has. This messenger is Spearmaster.

Ruined Experiment

After several cycle with no water and still no contact with Five Pebbles, Moon decides to forcefully send a message through to Pebbles. This results in him breaking focus and leads to the unfortunate development called the rot.

Spearmaster Delivery

Spearmaster delivers a pearl containing a message to Five Pebbles. After being throwed out by an angry Pebbles, Spearmaster shows the message to Looks to the Moon. She asks to write a new message so that Spearmaster can broadcast it at a communication tower nearby.

Moon's Message

Spearmaster broadcasts this final message from Moon to all Iterators. With all the damage she sustained from a lack of water, Moon collapses and has presumably died.

Garbage Wastes

In his early attempts to combat the rot, Pebbles tries flushing it out into an area called Garbage Wastes, but it proves resilient and continues to consume him.

Time Passes

An unknown, but significant amount of cycles pass. Looks to the Moon is deteriorating and Five Pebbles continues to be consumed by the rot.

The Hunter

The Hunter, a creature sent by the Iterator No Significant Harrassment, delivers a special package to Looks to the Moon. This package contains a green neuron fly which reawakens Looks to the Moon and her own neuron flies. Neuron flies play the role of brain cells for an Iterator.

Rivulet

Rivulet, a new aquatic-like creature, makes a journey to Five Pebbles. By now, the rot has spread to much of Five Pebbles and has taken a heavy toll on him. Pebbles expresses regret for his actions and asks Rivulet to bring his Rarefaction Cell to Looks to the Moon.

Battery Removal

Rivulet removes the Rarefaction Cell, causing Five Pebbles to lose significant power as it was his main battery.

Rivulet Delivery

Rivulet returns to Looks to the Moon with the cell. She asks Rivulet to enter her structure, which has now mostly sunken into the ocean. By doing so, Rivulet delivers the cell to Moon's core which allows Moon to finally have power.

Reconciliation

With partial restoration of power, Looks to the Moon regains control of her systems and initiates a private broadcast with Five Pebbles, seeking reconciliation.

Snowfall

So much time has passed that most Iterator activity has ceased. This change also affected the environment. Endless snowfall replaces the rain as little output from Iterators remain and many locations have decayed and changed over the passing of time.

Five Pebbles Falls

Five Pebbles is seen to have also fallen, due to a lack of power to support him. His exterior has cracked in half, leaving the majority of him to decay of the planet's cold surface.

Rotten God

Five Pebbles has outlived the rot, but is now left barely functional. A once declared god left to rot away for eternity.

Communication Lost

Looks to the Moon has recovered some and continues with her assigned task. She misses Five Pebbles and can no longer contact to him.

Ascension

In the finale, a creature named Saint is capable of ascending others without the use of Void Fluid. He can choose to ascend Looks to the Moon and Five Pebbles, freeing them of their eternal cycle, but this is never confirmed to be canon.
